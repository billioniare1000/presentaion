المقدمة

What is the car robot?

The robotic car is a comprehensive educational project that relies on Arduino technology to add intelligence to the vehicle's movement control, enabling it to perform various tasks. Key features of this car include:
1. Line Following: The car can detect and precisely follow black lines drawn on the ground.
2. Object Tracking: Equipped with sensors, the car can sense its surroundings and follow moving objects within a specific range.
3. Obstacle Avoidance: Thanks to its sensors, the car can detect obstacles in its path and change direction to avoid them.
4. Remote Control via Bluetooth: The car can be controlled remotely using a Bluetooth-enabled smartphone app, giving the user full control from a distance.
5.This project is an excellent opportunity to learn the basics of microcontroller programming, sensor usage, and integrating these elements into practical
applications that simulate technologies used in autonomous vehicles.

الانظمه

1- Bluetooth control

In this mode, the car is operated entirely via a Bluetooth connection with a smartphone or other Bluetooth-enabled device.

• Setup: The car connects to a smartphone app that sends directional commands (forward, backward, left, right, stop).

• User Control: Commands are sent in real time, allowing precise, immediate control. Users can adjust the car's direction, speed, and stop it instantly, ideal for hands-on control.

• Applications: This mode is great for remote exploration or driving in tight spaces. Users can test movement patterns or navigate around objects with ease.

• Challenges: Bluetooth range is limited, so this mode works best for close-range operation. Interference from other devices may also affect the connection.

2- object tracking mode

In object-tracking mode, the car follows a moving object (like a person or another vehicle) while maintaining a safe distance.

• Sensor Setup: The car uses ultrasonic or other distance sensors to measure the distance to the object in front.

• Object at Optimal Distance (e.g., 15-20 cm): The car stops to maintain a steady distance.

• Object Moves Farther Away: The car moves forward to close the distance.

• Object Moves Closer: The car moves backward to avoid collision.

• Applications: This mode is ideal for applications like security patrolling, where the car might follow a person or another vehicle while maintaining a fixed following distance.

• Challenges: The tracking effectiveness depends on sensor accuracy. Fast or sudden movements by the tracked object can be challenging for the car to match.

3- Line Following Mode

In line-following mode, the car automatically follows a black line on the ground, using infrared (IR) sensors to detect the line.

• Sensor Setup: IR sensors are mounted at the bottom of the car to detect the contrast between the black line and the surface around it.

• Behavior: The car reads sensor data to determine its position relative to the line:

• Centered on the Line: Moves forward.

• Left or Right Deviation: Adjusts direction slightly to realign with the line.

• Off Track: If the car veers off completely, it stops to prevent wandering away.

• Applications: Useful for automated delivery or guided navigation, this mode is common in warehouse robots and guided vehicles.

• Challenges: Sharp turns or inconsistent line visibility can cause issues, requiring good lighting and clear

4- Obstacle Avoidance Mode

Obstacle avoidance uses ultrasonic sensors to detect objects in the car’s path and avoid them automatically.

• Sensor Setup: Ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves that bounce back from obstacles, allowing the car to calculate distance.

• No Obstacle in Path: The car moves forward normally.

• Obstacle Detected Within a Certain Distance: The car stops, then decides on a new path

• Applications: Suitable for navigating unknown environments, obstacle avoidance allows the car to explore while staying safe.

• Challenges: Very narrow spaces or small obstacles may be hard to detect, as ultrasonic sensors work best on larger objects.

النتائج

After implementing the project, tangible results were achieved. Some of the key outcomes include:

  • The ability to detect the operating system from a phone using Bluetooth.
  • The car's ability to overcome obstacles efficiently and accurately.
  • The car's ability to maintain a safe distance from objects accurately and efficiently.
  • Achieving high efficiency in the car's ability to follow the black line.
  • The car responds to Bluetooth commands with high accuracy, efficiency, and speed without any delay.
